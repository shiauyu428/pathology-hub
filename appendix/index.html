HISTOLOGIC GRADING — CAP v5.1.0.0 / AJCC 9th
General Principles

Prognostic value: Histologic grade is a proven prognostic factor for appendiceal carcinoma.

Discordance rule: Appendiceal and peritoneal grades are usually concordant but may differ. When discordant, both must be recorded independently; the peritoneal tumor grade drives the final staging grade.

Mucinous Tumors — AJCC 3-Tier Scheme

Based on cytologic atypia, tumor cellularity, and signet-ring cell component.

G1 — Well-differentiated
  • Low-grade cytologic atypia; no signet-ring cells
  • Typically LAMN (pushing borders only, no destructive invasion)
  • Peritoneal involvement: acellular mucin or low cellularity (<20%), no infiltrative invasion
G2 — Moderately differentiated
  • Mixed low/high-grade or diffuse high-grade cytologic atypia; no signet-ring cells
  • Typically HAMN, or mucinous adenocarcinoma with destructive invasion and desmoplasia
  • Complex architecture: cribriform glands, complex papillary structures
  • Extra-appendiceal spread may show perineural or lymphovascular invasion
G3 — Poorly differentiated
  • High-grade cytologic atypia with signet-ring cell component (>10%)
  • Includes: pure signet-ring cell adenocarcinoma, high-grade goblet cell adenocarcinoma
  • Exclusion: Pseudosignet ring cells (degenerate cells floating in mucin) do not qualify as G3
⚠ Exceptions — Grading schemes above do NOT apply to:
  • Poorly differentiated neuroendocrine carcinoma
  • Goblet cell adenocarcinoma
  • Undifferentiated carcinoma (G4 is not included in AJCC 9th edition)
